# holographic — the whole is in every part

Where [[fractal]] is the same *form* at every scale, **holographic** is the whole *content* in every part: any part regenerates the whole, and the whole reflects in every part. The **content-[[identity|uuid]] is the hologram** — the id *is* the content, compressed into the address — so one part (a uuid, a row, a skill path) carries enough to reconstruct ([[merge]]) the whole from the [[akashic]] record (source → config → `payload-types.ts`, and every uuid-addressed row/file). A skill folder loads its own relations ([[aura]]); each agent *is* the whole erpax ([[self]]) — one node holds the pattern. Sequence position **0** (the axis — [[identity]]/[[config]]).

So you **forget freely**: drop the corpus from context, and the part regenerates what's needed on demand — minimal context, full reach (the [[duality]] learn↔forget). And disharmony anywhere surfaces everywhere — the `tsc` tail, the [[aura]] gap, the generated types — the whole watching through every part. Many parts at one coordinate ([[dimension]]) is the superposition; each still holds the whole.

Composes: [[fractal]] (form-twin — together self-similar AND self-containing) · [[identity]] (uuid encodes the whole) · [[akashic]] (regenerate) · [[self]] (agent = whole) · [[aura]] (folder = its hashed subgraph) · [[merge]]·[[one]] (parts reconstruct one whole) · [[dimension]].

## Common mistakes
- Storing the whole redundantly instead of regenerating it from a content-addressed part.
- Treating a part as lossy when content-addressing makes it whole-bearing (the uuid IS the content).
