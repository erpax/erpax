# pruning — removing growth to steer the plant

**pruning** is the removal of plant parts to steer growth, airflow, and fruiting — concentrating the plant's energy into **marketable fruit**, opening the canopy to cut disease, and shaping it to the [[trellis]]. On indeterminate tomatoes the key act is **suckering** — pinching the axillary "armpit" shoots at 2–4″ to limit the plant to one or two leaders. It governs the trade between vegetative growth and fruit, and between total count and fruit size/[[grade]].

Pruning is a recurring [[manufacturing|operation]] through the [[season]], its labor a real [[cost]], paired with [[trellis]] (you prune what you train) and informed by [[scouting]] (remove diseased tissue). Done right it lifts [[grade]] and [[yield]] quality; overdone it sheds the canopy that feeds the fruit.
