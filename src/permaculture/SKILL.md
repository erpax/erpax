# permaculture — designing the farm as a self-sustaining ecosystem

**permaculture** (permanent + agriculture) is a whole-system design philosophy — Bill Mollison & David Holmgren's framework — for arranging [[crop]]s, animals, water, and structures into a **self-sustaining ecosystem** whose outputs feed its own inputs (closed loops, minimal external import). Its three **ethics** — *earth care · people care · fair share* — and 12 principles (observe, catch & store energy, obtain a yield, produce no waste, use edges) sit *above* the individual operations: where [[tillage]] / [[irrigation]] / [[fertility]] are the verbs, permaculture is the design that places them. Tools: **zones** (by visit-frequency), **guilds** (mutually-supporting plant/animal groupings), **stacking** (multiple yields per space), and water earthworks (swales, keyline).

It is the design twin of erpax's own laws — closed-loop ([[breath]]: no [[balance|waste]], outputs = inputs), [[whole]]-system, [[fractal]] (zones nest) — and the broader frame **agroecology** (FAO) generalizes to. The produce of a permaculture system often carries [[organic]] / regenerative [[certification]].

**Law — [[law]]: design the farm as a closed-loop [[whole]] where the system's outputs feed its own inputs (no waste, [[balance]]d) — the agronomic twin of the [[breath]].**

## Standards
- Mollison & Holmgren — *Permaculture One* (1978); Holmgren — *Permaculture: Principles & Pathways Beyond Sustainability* (the 12 principles, 3 ethics)
- FAO — agroecology (the 10 elements); Savory Institute — holistic management / EOV (Land to Market)
- Regenerative Organic Certified; Yeomans keyline design
