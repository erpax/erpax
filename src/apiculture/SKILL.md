# apiculture — beekeeping for produce and pollination

**apiculture** is the keeping of honey-bee colonies for produce and pollination — a living-production domain anchor beside [[agriculture]] and [[livestock]]. The **colony** (a queen + workers, its strength a [[biomass]] proxy) is the [[biological/assets|biological asset]]; the **hive** that houses it is IAS-16 equipment (the bearer/equipment carve-out, mirroring the bearer-plant trap). **Honey** and **wax** are the [[harvest|harvested]] produce ([[items|inventory]] at point of harvest).

The colony's life maps the living-production atoms: **brood** is its growth ([[maturity]]), **swarming** and nucleus splits its [[propagation]], **colony loss** its [[mortality]], the **nectar flow** its [[season]]. Crucially, the colony is also **rented as a [[pollination]] service** — a service revenue distinct from honey, and the direct link binding apiculture to the crop cluster's fruit-set. [[health]] (mites/disease) and forage range gate colony strength.

## Standards
- IFRS IAS-41 / IAS-16 (bee colony as biological asset; hive as equipment); FAO apiculture
- Land-grant apiculture extension (colony management, pollination contracts); pollination-service rates

Composes [[agriculture]] · [[livestock]] · [[biological/assets]] · [[biomass]] · [[harvest]] · [[items]] · [[maturity]] · [[propagation]] · [[mortality]] · [[season]] · [[pollination]] · [[health]] · [[yield]].

**Law — [[law]]: the bee colony is the IAS-41 biological asset while the hive housing it is IAS-16 equipment (the bearer carve-out); honey and wax are harvested produce, and the colony also earns pollination-service revenue distinct from produce.**
