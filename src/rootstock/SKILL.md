# rootstock — the root system a scion is grafted onto

A **rootstock** is the root-providing lower plant onto which a fruiting [[scion]] is joined by a [[graft]] — the structural keystone of a [[perennial]] planting. It controls **vigour and size** (dwarfing rootstocks enable high-density, early-bearing orchards), **disease and pest resistance** (e.g. phylloxera-resistant grape rootstocks), and adaptation to [[soil]]. Chosen **once** for the asset's multi-decade productive life, it is a capitalized part of the [[biological/assets|bearer-plant]] asset.

The rootstock × [[scion]] pairing is the perennial's genetic architecture — the woody-crop analog of the annual's [[seed]] choice, but clonal (vegetatively propagated, [[propagation]]) and permanent. It sets the [[spacing]]/density the planting will hold and the [[maturity]] (time-to-bearing) ramp.

## Standards
- Extension pomology & viticulture (rootstock selection, dwarfing, disease resistance)
- FPS/UC Davis (clean-plant rootstock); IFRS IAS-16 (the planting as depreciable PP&E)
