# perennial — a crop that lives and bears across many years

A **perennial** is a [[crop]] that lives and produces across many years — the woody, multi-decade kind (orchard, vineyard, cane, olive) that contrasts with the annual cluster. It is **established once** (a multi-year non-bearing period whose costs capitalize), **ramps** over years to full [[yield]], **bears** for decades, then is **renewed** (replanted). Its defining accounting fact is the **bearer-plant carve-out**: the productive plant is a depreciating fixed asset (IAS-16 PP&E, amortized over its productive life — see [[biological/assets]] · [[impairment]]); only the **produce growing on it** stays IAS-41 at fair value.

This inverts five annual assumptions: the plant is a **standing depreciable asset** (not within-season inventory); a multi-year **yield ramp** replaces single-season yield; **renewal/replant** replaces [[rotation]]; [[spacing]] is **permanent** for the asset's life; and [[season]] recurs on a standing plant ([[dormancy]] → bud-break → [[maturity|veraison]] → [[harvest]]) rather than re-instantiating the [[crop]]. Propagated clonally ([[rootstock]] + [[scion]] via [[graft]]), placed by [[terroir]].

## Standards
- IFRS IAS-41 §5A / IAS-16 (the 2014 bearer-plant amendment — plant is PP&E, produce is IAS-41)
- IRC §263A(d) (pre-productive cost capitalization); extension pomology/viticulture cost studies
