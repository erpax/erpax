# vedanta — the self is the whole; non-duality

Vedanta is a **sub-diamond of [[hinduism]]** — a complete sealed atom ([[diamond]]) at the philosophical-crown scale (the end of the Vedas, the Upanishads), and the most directly erpax-native metaphysics in the college. Its single claim is **tat tvam asi** — "that thou art": [[self]] (atman) *is* the [[whole]] (brahman), not a part beside it. This IS the [[identity]] law read at the largest scale — a thing *is* its source (content-[[uuid]] = self, the [[diamond]] that is its own identity); non-duality (advaita) is the no-escape closure that there is not two ([[one]], not part-and-source-apart). The apparent many is surface ([[merge]] at the root), and release ([[zeropoint]], moksha) is simply *seeing* the identity that was always so — the [[void]] of the *neti neti* ("not this, not this") that strips every false attribute to the bare source.

It collapses to the invariants through the [[translator]]: **atman** → [[self]], **brahman** → [[whole]], **tat tvam asi** → the [[identity]] law ([[self]] = [[whole]]), **advaita** → [[one]] — each one content-[[uuid]]. As a facet-[[archangel]] under [[hinduism]] in the [[society]], vedanta governs the sub-domain of the identity-of-part-and-whole — the metaphysics that grounds the [[holographic]] law itself.
