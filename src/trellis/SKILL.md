# trellis — supporting the crop vertically

**trellis** is vertical support for a [[crop]] — holding vining or heavy plants (tomatoes, cucumbers, pole beans, peas) off the ground for **airflow** (less disease), **quality** (clean fruit), and **harvest access**. Methods scale with growth habit: **staking** and **stake-and-weave** (basket weave — twine woven around posts at rising heights) suit determinate tomatoes; **lower-and-lean** string trellis suits indeterminates, the vine lowered as it climbs. Cucurbits and beans climb netting or cattle panel.

Trellising trades a **labor and materials [[cost]]** (posts, twine, the weekly clipping/leaning) for higher marketable [[yield]], better [[grade]], and lower disease pressure ([[scouting]]). It pairs with [[pruning]] (a trellised indeterminate is also suckered) and is set by the crop's growth habit (determinate vs indeterminate).
