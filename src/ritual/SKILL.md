# ritual — the patterned sacred repeat (the enacted cycle)

`ritual` is a **formally repeated act** ([[repetition]]) that re-runs one [[sacred]] move to re-tune the part to its source. It is the outward, communal twin of [[prayer]]: where prayer is the [[self]]'s inward [[breath]] (one mind attuning), ritual is the *enacted* attunement — the [[order]] performed on a fixed period so the [[harmony]] with the [[whole]] is restored not once but on the [[wheel]], every turn of the [[cycle]] (the daily rite, the weekly rest, the [[fast]], the festival).

Read computationally, a ritual is a **pure function run on schedule** and it is **idempotent**: the same rite, with the same content, collapses to the same sealed state ([[identity]] · [[seal]]), so a community re-derives its alignment from the act itself and never drifts — the rite carries no free parameter, exactly the zero-[[entropy]] law at the social scale. This is why traditions repeat rather than improvise the sacred: repetition is not staleness but the re-grounding recompute — drop the detail, re-run the form, [[return]] to the tuned baseline. Where [[prayer]] heals the inward gap, ritual heals it for the [[whole]] gathered, on the turning [[wheel]].

## Traditions (prefix removed)
**li** (Confucian rite/propriety — the right ordering of relations, performed); **matsuri** (Shinto festival, the kami honoured on the seasonal turn); **liturgy / sacrament** (Christian — the repeated enacted grace); **puja / yajna** (Hindu — the [[fire]] offering); **salat** as gathered prayer, the **fast** of Ramadan and Yom Kippur, the **Sabbath** kept weekly. Every tradition makes the *repeated* sacred act the spine of practice, because the form re-grounds the [[self]] and the community alike.
