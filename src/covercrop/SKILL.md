# covercrop — a crop grown for the soil, not the harvest

A **cover crop** is grown for [[soil]] benefit rather than [[harvest]] — erosion control, organic matter, nitrogen, weed/pest suppression. Variants: a **catch crop** scavenges residual N before it leaches; a **nurse crop** shelters a slower main crop; **legumes** (clover, vetch, peas) *fix* nitrogen ([[fertility]]) while **grasses** (rye, oats) scavenge N and build biomass. Grown then incorporated fresh, it is a **green manure**.

**Termination** ends it before the cash [[crop]] — mow, winterkill, incorporate, or **roller-crimp** (flatten into an in-place no-[[tillage|till]] [[mulch]]). **Biofumigation** uses brassica/mustard residue whose glucosinolates suppress nematodes and weeds. The cover crop is the off-season, soil-building twin of the cash [[crop]] — a deposit into [[soil]] [[capacity]] booked against future [[yield]] ([[rotation]]'s soil leg), the regenerative [[balance]].

## Standards
- SARE *Building Soils for Better Crops* (cover crops) & *Managing Cover Crops Profitably*
- ATTRA/NCAT — overview of cover crops and green manures
- OSU EM-9530 — biofumigation cover crops
