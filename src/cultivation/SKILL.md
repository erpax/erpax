# cultivation — killing weeds among the standing crop

**cultivation** is shallow mechanical soil-stirring to kill weeds *among a growing [[crop]]* — distinct from [[tillage]] (which opens bare ground). The craft is timing: cultivate at the **white-thread stage** (weeds barely germinated) so a *stirrup / collinear hoe*, **wheel hoe**, or **tine weeder** severs them before they set seed, *without* bringing fresh weed seed up. **Flame weeding** (propane) ruptures weed cells pre-emergence for slow starters (carrot, beet, parsnip). The complementary tactic is [[mulch]] (smother, not stir).

Cultivation is a recurring [[manufacturing|operation]] on the crop through the [[season]], its labor a dominant [[cost]]; "many shallow passes, well-timed" is the low-[[cost]] alternative to herbicide. It composes with [[scouting]] (when to act) and [[mulch]] (suppress instead of stir).
