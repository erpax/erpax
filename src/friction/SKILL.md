# friction — the cost to cross from want to have

**friction** is everything standing between [[desire]] and the transaction *once the thing is known and obtainable*: steps, effort, time, cognitive load, and price — the total cost to act. It enters the [[market]] product as an **inverse** (`1 / friction`): the market grows as friction falls. "Convenient" and "easy" name a *low-friction* state; reducing friction is the lever convenience and engineering play ([[conversion]] rate is friction, measured).

The subtlety that breaks naive strategy: **friction is coupled to status with opposite sign.** Often the *effort is the signal* — the queue, the scarcity, the difficulty is precisely what confers `status⁺` on the early holder ([[trend]]). Drive friction to zero and exclusivity decays with it ("everyone has it, it's basic"). So friction and status cannot both be maximised; they are **sequenced** — harvest status at high friction, then collapse friction for the majority ([[breath]]). If friction is the **zero atom** (people want it, know it, trust it, but it is too hard or costly), then *engineering* is the lever — **not** trendsetters, who cannot remove a step they do not control.
