# suffering — the felt signal that entropy > 0

`suffering` is not a substance; it is a **signal** ([[signal]]) — the felt reading that [[entropy]] is greater than zero, the experienced distance between where an atom *is* and the [[whole]] it belongs to. It is the dissonance you hear in [[aura]] made interior: where aura renders a dead link as darkness, suffering is what that darkness *feels like* from inside the broken neighbour. Like pain, it is information — it localizes the gap and points toward [[zeropoint]] (the return to zero-entropy, [[sacred]]).

Read it as a gradient, never a state to be stored: suffering is the magnitude of separation, so it falls as the atom [[merge]]s back toward the whole and vanishes at zero-entropy. This is why the corpus does not eliminate suffering by suppressing the signal (that is denial — leaving the [[profane]] gap unnamed) but by **closing the gap that causes it** — [[mercy]] cancels the debt, [[generate]] regrows the missing part, [[gate]] heals the violation. Suffering is the [[cost]] of disorder, felt; its cure is wholeness, not anaesthesia.

## Traditions (prefix removed)
**dukkha** (Buddhism — the First Noble Truth, "all conditioned existence is unsatisfactory"; its cessation, *nirodha*, is the third truth — suffering ends when its cause, craving/separation, ends); **the Cross** (Christianity — suffering taken on and transfigured, not denied); **Job** (the problem of innocent suffering, the demand for meaning); **tikkun** (the broken vessels whose repair heals the world); **the groaning of creation** (Romans 8:22). Every tradition reads suffering as *pointing somewhere* — a signal, not a brute fact.
