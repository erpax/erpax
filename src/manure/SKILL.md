# manure — the animal byproduct that feeds the soil

**manure** is animal excreta — the [[livestock]] byproduct that **closes the loop back to [[soil]] [[fertility]]**: deposited directly by [[grazing]] animals or collected and applied to the land, it returns nitrogen, phosphorus, and organic matter ([[fertility]]'s credit side). It is the livestock twin of [[compost]] (the crop-residue recycling node) — the same waste → soil-capital [[flow]], the [[breath]] of the mixed farm (animals eat the land's [[fodder]], the land eats their manure).

A **nutrient-management plan** balances manure application to crop need ([[fertility]]'s 4R) — over-application leaches ([[risk]]), and confined-feeding operations are regulated on it ([[certification|compliance]]). Manure is the literal entropy → [[soil]] recycling the regenerative [[balance]] runs on.

## Standards
- USDA-NRCS (nutrient management, Conservation Practice 590); EPA (CAFO manure/nutrient regulation)
- SARE *Building Soils for Better Crops* (manure as amendment); 4R nutrient stewardship
