# market — the emergent whole where desire meets supply (a product of atoms)

A **market** is not a place and not a sum — it is the **product** of its atoms. A transaction exists only when *every* gate is open at once: someone wants it ([[desire]]), knows it exists ([[awareness]]), can obtain it ([[distribution]]), can afford the effort and price ([[friction]]), believes it works ([[proof]]), is socially permitted-or-rewarded to hold it (the social sign — *status*), and comes back ([[habit]]). Multiplicatively:

```
market = desire × awareness × distribution × (1 / friction) × proof × status⁺ × habit
```

Because it is a **product, not a sum**, a *small* market means **at least one atom is near zero** — and the weakest atom caps all the rest ([[bottleneck]] · [[constraint]]: multiply a larger number by the zero and it is still zero). Growth is therefore not "do more of everything" but **find the zero atom and lift it**. Pumping a non-zero atom while the zero stays zero is the universal market trap — bigger number times zero is zero.

**The trendsetter operator.** Those who *lead* trends ([[trend]]) are not a generic demand boost — they are the one actor that can flip the social sign from `−` (stigma / indifference) to `+` (aspiration), and seed [[awareness]] as taste-amplifiers ([[power]] · [[signal]]). So trendsetters are the *right* lever **only when the zero atom is status or awareness**. If the zero is [[friction]] you need engineering, not influence; if [[proof]], they help only as credible endorsement (proof-by-association); if [[desire]], nothing reaches it — you must redefine the product.

**The coupling (why "convenient *and* exclusive" is an oxymoron).** status and friction are **not independent** — they are coupled with opposite sign. Often the effort *is* the signal; the scarcity *is* the status. Lower friction (make it convenient) and `status⁺` decays toward `0` — "everyone has it now, it's basic." You cannot maximise both at once. You **sequence** them — the [[breath]], run on demand, a double [[entry]]:

1. **Inhale** — harvest `status⁺` while friction is high: rare, exclusive, *be-first*. The trendsetters pay the friction *because* it signals.
2. **Exhale** — lower friction for the majority, accepting that status will fade, betting [[habit]] + [[awareness]] now carry the volume (crossing from the few to the many).

You do not win convenience and status simultaneously; you win them **in order**. erpax is itself a [[society]] whose [[commerce]] is many such markets — the same law governs adoption of an atom, a feature, or a [[trend]].

**The static law is a snapshot; [[diffusion]] is its time axis.** The product above says *why* a market is big or small right now — it does not say how a small one *becomes* big. That is a contagion over time (Bass: word-of-mouth `q ≈ 0.38` far exceeds advertising `p ≈ 0.03`), seeded by the trendsetter 16% and stalled at the [[diffusion|chasm]] — where most small markets stay small. Where [[desire]] itself grows with adoption it is a [[network]] effect: the strongest small-to-big engine and, below critical mass, the cold-start trap. Read [[diffusion]] and [[network]] for the dynamics.

## Standards

- **Diffusion of Innovations** — Everett Rogers (1962): the adopter curve (innovators → early adopters → early/late majority → laggards); the trendsetters are the left tail.
- **Crossing the Chasm** — Geoffrey Moore (1991): the gap between early adopters (want to be first — status) and the early majority (want proof and convenience). The [[breath]] above is the bridge across it.
- **AIDA funnel** — awareness → interest → desire → action: the per-atom gates read as a sequence.
