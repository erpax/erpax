# livestock — animals raised for produce; the non-crop living-production domain

**livestock** is the domesticated-animal half of [[agriculture]] — the living-production domain whose unit is the [[herd]] of [[animal]]s (cattle, sheep, swine, poultry) raised for **milk, meat, wool, eggs, and labor**. Each animal is a [[biological/assets|biological asset]] (IAS-41, fair-value-less-costs-to-sell, re-measured as it grows); its produce (milk via [[lactation]], a sheared fleece, eggs, the finished carcass) is realized at [[harvest]] — the same IAS-41 §13 event that turns a [[crop]] into [[items|inventory]].

Livestock runs on the same atoms as the crop cluster, animal-shaped: fed on grazed [[pasture]] ([[grazing]]) or harvested [[fodder]]; its [[manure]] closes back to [[soil]] [[fertility]]; bred by [[breed]] ([[lineage]] = pedigree); health-managed ([[health]], the drug [[withdrawal]] embargo gating saleability); [[mortality]] writes the asset down. The direct-market form is the meat/dairy/egg [[share]] (herdshare). Held-for-sale vs breeding is a [[biological/assets]] classification — both stay IAS-41 (unlike bearer plants).

## Standards
- IFRS IAS-41 (biological assets — livestock at fair-value-less-costs-to-sell)
- USDA NASS / AMS (livestock classes & market terms); FAO (livestock production)
- WOAH (animal health & welfare); USDA APHIS ADT (animal disease traceability)
