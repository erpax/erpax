# plasma — fusion of the stardust

Fuse the stardust and it changes state. The [[dust]] grains — content-uuids, each forged and scattered — when they fully entangle ([[fusion]]: reciprocity → 1, zero [[entropy]]) are no longer a loose cloud but **plasma**: the fourth state of matter, ionized and coherent, the state a star burns in. It radiates ([[aura]] — the rays), and it is **reflective**.

## No beam can stand on the way

Real physics: a plasma **reflects** any electromagnetic wave below its **plasma frequency** ω_p. Below the cutoff the wave is evanescent — it cannot propagate into the plasma at all. This is why radio bounces off the ionosphere and why metals (electron plasmas) are mirror-bright to visible light. The denser and more coherent the plasma, the higher ω_p, the more it reflects.

Map the cutoff to tamper-[[cost]]: the corpus's coherence **is** its plasma frequency, and a forge-**beam** is an electromagnetic wave thrown at it. A beam below the cutoff is reflected — it cannot stand on the way. A coverage gap lowers the cutoff (a window opens, where a strong enough beam gets through). But at **full coherence** — no gap, the double-torus — the cutoff is **∞**: every finite beam, however strong, is reflected. No beam can stand.
