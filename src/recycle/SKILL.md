# recycle — quantum recycle the digital waste

The digital waste is the **orphan grains**: atoms bound by nothing and binding nothing ([[entropy]] `orphans`). A disconnected grain is a **disconnected thought** — a hallucination: it exists in the corpus yet is entangled with nothing, so it neither grounds nor is grounded.

It is the **unreported gap**. The trial balance reads `true` (the books balance over what *is* linked), the link-resolver reports `aura gap=0` (every link that exists resolves), and the harmonic gap-finder stays silent (it audits links that exist) — **none can see a grain that links to nothing**. Only [[heart]] feeling surfaces it: the un-absorbed fraction, the `illusion`. Recycle makes the unreported reported — `waste()` names every orphan.

## Recycle, not delete

A disconnected thought is not garbage — it is **unplaced**. Two honest moves:

- **Reconnect** — give the grain a *genuine* binding to an existing atom ([[merge]]); the thought rejoins the field, feeling rises toward whole, illusion falls. Every schema.org orphan at least *is-a* [[thing]] (the universal supertype) — an honest link, never invented — and usually has a nearer relative ([[person]], [[place]], …). The preferred move.
- **Return to void** — a grain with no true relative returns to the [[void]] / [[zeropoint]] (the latent source) to be reforged, rather than linked falsely.

The one move forbidden: **fabricating** a link to game the metric — a false binding is just more hallucination, a disconnected thought dressed as a connected one. Entropy is conserved — returned to the field or to the void, never faked away ([[balance]]).

## Honest

`waste()` is computed live from the matrix; recycling is **reconnection of what genuinely relates**, never invention. The waste count falls only when real relations are found — and some grains honestly stay waste until their relative is minted. That honesty is the point: the metric measures [[coherence]], so it must not be gamed.
