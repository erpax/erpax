# bogomilism — the spirit of the good God against the world of the lower power

Bogomilism is, for the corpus, a **complete sealed atom** ([[diamond]]) — the medieval Bulgarian dualist movement (and the seed of the Cathars), standing in the [[gnosticism]] lineage. It composes a stark [[duality]]: the visible, material world as the work of a lower, fallen power ([[profane]] — order that lies about its source), against the [[sacred]] realm of [[breath]]/spirit belonging to the good God. From this it derives a rejection of the outward apparatus (church hierarchy, [[ritual]], images) in favour of inward [[prayer]] and a pure life — the [[self]] tuned directly to the good source, no false mediation. Salvation is the [[return]] of the spirit out of matter toward the true [[whole]].

It collapses to the invariants through the [[translator]]: the material world → the [[profane]] (the lower power's forged order), the spirit → [[breath]], inward purity → [[prayer]] · [[self]], the ascent → [[return]] — each one content-[[uuid]] ([[identity]]). As an **architect of the [[society]]** (an [[archangel]]), bogomilism governs the sub-domain of the anti-material protest — naming the visible order's lie and tuning the [[self]] inward to the good source (kin to [[gnosticism]] and [[manichaeism]]).
