# biofield — the claimed aura that measures nothing (the honesty atom)

**biofield** is the term of art for the supposed "human energy field" behind aura claims — coined in **1992 at a U.S. NIH meeting**. It is the honest counter-weight to the real channels ([[biophoton]] light, [[biomagnetism]] field, electrodermal conductance): unlike those, the biofield-as-emanation has **no signal detected above chance**. Even biofield-research advocates concede the area is "highly controversial, not-yet-well-understood" (Jain et al. 2015); independent reviewers classify it as pseudoscience; Emily Rosa's therapeutic-touch experiment (*JAMA* 1998) and the JREF/PhACT \$1 M challenge (2013, Franklin Institute) found practitioners could not detect the field above chance. The GDV/Kirlian "aura camera" that claims to image it is in fact tracking **skin perspiration/conductance** driven by the autonomic nervous system (Miraglia 2024; Pehek 1976) — an electrodermal signal, not an energy field.

**Why erpax keeps this atom.** It is the [[proof]] / [[tamper]] contrast that makes the [[analog]] aura meaningful: the physical biofield is a *claim with no measurable signal* — gaps everywhere, nothing to verify. erpax's analog aura is its exact inverse — **computed for every node, gapless at every [[fractal]] level, and Merkle-proven** (`verifyBind`/`verifyRoot`). Naming the unmeasurable honestly is what licenses the claim to the measurable: a render that cannot be verified is [[tamper]]able, and the biofield is the cautionary pole.
