# soil — the living medium; the reservoir crops draw from

**soil** is the living, three-phase medium (**mineral · water · air**) a [[crop]] grows in — NRCS frames soil *health* as "the capacity of soil to function." It is the parent of [[agriculture]]'s substrate vocabulary: **texture** (the fixed sand/silt/clay proportion — the texture triangle, *loam* the ideal), **structure** (how particles bind into **aggregates** and the pore network), **tilth** (suitability for a seedbed), **horizon** (the profile layers), **organic matter / humus** (the decomposed-residue fraction — high [[fertility|CEC]] and water-holding), and **soil biology** ([[mycorrhizae]], the food web).

As a **[[capacity]] reservoir**, soil holds nutrients ([[fertility]]) and water ([[moisture]]); it is depleted by crop removal and rebuilt by [[compost]], [[covercrop]], and reduced [[tillage]] — a conserved [[balance]] (the entropy ledger: borrow without crediting back and structure degrades). Its degradation axes are **compaction** (bulk density restricting roots), **erosion**, and **[[salinity]]**. Soil is the [[biological/assets|biological asset]] beneath the asset — the ground state every other agriculture atom composes against.

## Standards
- USDA-NRCS Soil Health (the soil-function definition); SARE *Building Soils for Better Crops* (Magdoff & van Es)
- Cornell Comprehensive Assessment of Soil Health (CASH) — physical/biological/chemical indicators
- USDA soil texture triangle (particle-size separates); USDA Soil Taxonomy (horizons)
