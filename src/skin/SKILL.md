# skin — the boundary (the trust membrane)

The skin is the body's **largest organ** (≈1.2–2.2 m², ~10% of body mass) and its **interface with the world** — and erpax's boundary *is* the skin: the [[sandbox]]/[[gate]] membrane between the self and the outside. Three properties of the living skin map — as a *structural isomorphism*, each computed in the matter-twin — onto that boundary:

## 1. Barrier — selectively permeable
The integument is a **highly effective physical barrier against infectious agents and dehydration**, the stratum corneum a "brick-and-mortar" wall of shed corneocytes (StatPearls; Physiopedia). It **permits the sanctioned and blocks the threat** — exactly the trust boundary: only capability-bearing, credential-brokered calls pass the [[sandbox]]; pathogens (un-sanctioned calls) are refused.

## 2. Regenerates — self-renewal from seed
The epidermis **renews completely on a ~28-day cycle** — basal stem cells divide and push upward, ~1 layer/day shed and replaced (Clinikally; biologyinsights). Damage the barrier and it **rebuilds from the seed** ([[regeneration]]): the boundary is never permanent matter, always a regenerated projection — wound it and it heals.

## 3. Homeostasis — negative feedback to a setpoint
The skin is central to **thermoregulation** — keeping core temperature within bounds despite the outside (LibreTexts). Computed as negative feedback, the regulator **converges monotonically to the 37 °C setpoint** from above or below: the self held within bounds, the gate pulling any deviation back toward whole.
