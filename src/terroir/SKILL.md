# terroir — the place-as-value: where land gives produce its character

**terroir** is the area-specific interaction of [[soil]], climate ([[degreeday]] heat, [[frost]], [[dormancy]] chill), topography, and grower practice that gives a [[perennial]]/viticultural product its distinctive character (OIV Resolution VITI 333/2010). It is the **place-as-value** — the reason fruit/wine from one delimited place commands a premium and a land-value the asset's [[impairment]] test must respect.

Terroir is made legal as an **appellation of origin** — AVA (US/TTB), AOC, DOC, or EU **PDO/PGI** — a [[certification]] bound to a delimited [[territory]] that gates label use (e.g. ≥85% of grapes from the AVA). It is the woody-crop twin of [[lineage|provenance]]: identity and value flowing from *where*, not just *what*.

## Standards
- OIV Resolution VITI 333/2010 (definition of terroir); TTB 27 CFR Part 9 (AVA)
- EU Reg 1151/2012 (PDO/PGI); INAO (AOC)
