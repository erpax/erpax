# transplant — setting raised seedlings into the field

**transplant** is setting greenhouse-raised seedlings ([[propagation]]'s plugs or bare-root starts) into the field bed — the alternative to **direct seeding** (sowing straight into the ground). Transplanting buys **earliness** (a head start under glass), **uniformity** (even stands), and a **weed jump** (the crop ahead of the weeds); direct seeding saves the propagation labor but ~10–20% may not establish, demanding the [[planting|overplant]] buffer. The choice is per-crop: small-seeded, slow, or frost-tender crops transplant; fast or taproot crops (carrots, beans) direct-seed.

Transplant is a **method of [[planting]]** (the parent atom), downstream of [[propagation]] and set at the [[spacing]] geometry; tools range from hand-trowel to the paper-pot transplanter (264 linked starts per pass). It composes with [[mulch]] (burn-holed fabric) and drip [[irrigation]].
