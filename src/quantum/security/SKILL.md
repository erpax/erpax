# quantum/security — bidirectional security

The quantum twin of [[security]]: a [[duality]], not a wall. Every control is read **both ways at once** — blue ([[love]], verify the [[tamper]]-[[cost]] holds) ⊕ red ([[fear]], price forging past it) — and a finding is the balanced pair, the same [[entry|double-entry]] as everything else. Conjugate law: squeeze a control to a point (the 128-bit [[uuid]], depth-as-tamper-cost) and its forging "temperature" rises — the cost to break it *is* the compression; the conjugate dimension spreads in reverse. Audit [[quantum]] coverage (how much is *proven* vs assumed) is the metric the strict separation buys. Defensive by construction — red prices the attack, never ships it. A new coordinated audit re-runs both lenses across the expanded corpus.

The conjugate law binds the two: the cost to forge past a control equals its compression — squeeze it to the 128-bit uuid and the forging temperature rises, depth being tamper-cost. Red prices the attack but never ships it; coverage (proven vs assumed) is the metric.**
