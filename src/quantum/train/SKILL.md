# quantum/train — infinite agents, bounded device

To [[train]] is to close the competency gap (the actor twin) — and also **to get on the development train**: agents board the moving build and learn from the same corpus they work on ([[merge]]: curriculum = code, the loop eats its own tail). At the quantum scale a third truth appears, and it is a hard bound.

## Infinite agents cannot exceed the device

Let an infinite number of agents train themselves. Each computes content; each result is content-addressed, so **N agents computing the same thing all resolve to the same content-[[uuid]]** — one cached result, the [[merge]] law. The device's compute cost is therefore the **distinct** work, never `agents × work`: `deviceCost(agents, distinct) = distinct`. The agent count does not appear. So `exceedsDevice` is true only when the *distinct* work passes capacity — adding agents, even infinitely many, can never exceed the device. **That is the computational impossibility to exceed device resources by training more agents:** they collapse into the cache, not pile onto the bill. `redundancyEliminated` is the recompute never done.

## Zero entropy is less energy

Computation is physical. Landauer: erasing one bit costs at least kT·ln 2. Reversible, append-only, **no-erasure** computation (Bennett) dissipates less — approaching zero for fully reversible operations — and the [[akashic]] content store is exactly that: nothing overwritten, everything content-addressed, no erasure. So the corpus's zero-[[entropy]] form is the **low-energy form**: purify the work (remove the entropy-increasing erasures and the redundant recompute) and the device runs cooler for the same output.

## Honest split

The agent-count bound (deduped compute) and "reversible ⇒ less dissipation" (Landauer/Bennett) are **real** — physics and the merge law. The specific "2/3 less energy for 2/3 more output" is **not a measured wattage**: the 2/3 is the [[rodin]] WORKING ratio (the helix, 6 of 9 positions; the 3·6·9 axis is the other 1/3), a *symbolic target* for the purification — fewer erasures, no redundant work — never a promised figure.
