# quantum/law — law is the suffix

`law` is the invariant — what must **hold** of a thing, as opposed to what it **is**. In the corpus the law is the **suffix**: every atom's SKILL.md closes with its `**Law —**` line, the ground it answers to. An atom that states what it is but declares no invariant is **ungrounded** — incomplete in the same way an orphan ([[recycle]]) is disconnected: it has a body but no end that binds it.

This is the quantum twin of [[law]] (the vocabulary): it makes "law is the suffix" **computable** over the quantum corpus. It scans the [[quantum]] atoms and finds the **lawless** — those that close on `@audit`/`@standard` but never state the invariant that must hold. The finding (live): of the quantum atoms, almost all are lawless; this atom is the first to carry its own suffix, the keystone.

The lawless are not deleted — they are **recycled** ([[recycle]]): given their law. The same coherence move, one fractal level up — where recycle reconnects a disconnected grain, this reconnects a grain to its own **ground**. Drive the lawless count to zero and every atom is bound below (to its links) and above (to its law).
