# scouting — monitoring fields to drive the pest decision

**scouting** is the systematic, regular field-walk that monitors pest, disease, and crop status — the backbone of **integrated pest management (IPM)**. The loop: **observe & identify**, **count** the pest against an **economic threshold** (the level where control [[cost]] < projected damage), then **act** only when warranted — escalating cultural → biological (**beneficials**) → mechanical (exclusion netting, [[cultivation]]) → chemical as a last resort. It turns pest control from a calendar-spray into a **measured decision** ([[measure]] against a [[limit]]).

IPM itself is not a single atom but a [[workflow]] assembling existing ones — scouting (monitor) + [[risk]]/[[measure]] (assess) + [[limit]] (threshold) + [[cultivation]]/[[mulch]] (act); scouting is the missing field verb. It is the [[agriculture]] instance of the universal observe→decide loop — the same [[risk]] management the rest of the society runs.

## Standards
- EPA — IPM principles; land-grant extension (Clemson, Alabama, UMass) — scouting & economic thresholds
- USDA-NRCS Pest Management (Conservation Practice 595); eOrganic — organic IPM
