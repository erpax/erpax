# pollination — pollen transfer that sets the fruit

**pollination** is the transfer of pollen that enables **fruit and seed set** — required by fruiting [[crop]]s (cucurbits, tomatoes, peppers, squash, melon) to turn flowers into marketable [[harvest]]. Agents: **insects** (managed honeybees and bumblebees, plus wild native pollinators), **wind** (corn, beets), or **hand** (greenhouse tomatoes, hybrid [[seed]] production). Poor pollination is a hidden [[yield]] cap — misshapen cucurbits and blossom drop trace to it.

Pollination is an **ecosystem service** the farm must protect (pollinator habitat, no insecticide during bloom — the [[scouting]]/IPM link) or provide (rented hives, [[tunnel]] bumblebee colonies). It is a [[yield]] prerequisite upstream of [[maturity]], and the reason flowering-crop [[planting]] and pesticide timing must respect bloom.

## Standards
- USDA — pollinator-health guidance; land-grant extension — pollination of vegetable crops (hive stocking rates)
- Xerces Society — pollinator conservation; FAO — pollination services
