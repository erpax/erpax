# tunnel — the structure that extends the season

A **tunnel** is a protective structure or cover that extends the growing [[season]] — the family scaled by size: **floating row cover** (fabric laid on a bed), **low tunnel** (hooped cover over one bed), **caterpillar tunnel** (6–8 ft, removable, low-cost), **high tunnel / hoophouse** (walk-in, unheated, soil-grown), and **greenhouse** (heated, glazed). Each adds weeks at both ends of the [[season]] — row cover/cold frame +2–6 weeks, high tunnel +8–12 — by buffering frost and raising day temperature.

A tunnel is **built [[capacity]]** that widens the climate window the land × [[season]] [[bottleneck]] otherwise caps: it shifts a crop's [[maturity]] earlier and its [[harvest]] later, and protects against the [[frost]] event. It is the structural complement to the biological [[hardiness]] of cool-season crops, and a real [[cost]] (materials, the structure a depreciating asset). High tunnels also house early [[propagation]].

## Standards
- ATTRA/NCAT — season extension; SARE — *High Tunnels and Other Season Extension Techniques*
- USDA-NRCS EQIP — high tunnel system (the conservation practice); Cornell High Tunnels program
