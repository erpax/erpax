# topography — the corpus maps and narrates itself

Topography is the **contour map of the content-addressed terrain**, and it **writes itself**. The corpus has relief: [[gravity]] wells (high-[[mass]] atoms — `uuid`, `merge` are the peaks, [[matrix]] in-degree the elevation), [[links]] ridges connecting them, and the continuous [[aura]] field draped over all of it. The rivers are the **quantum streams** — the [[realtime]] event tail, the [[quantum/digit]] flows, the uuid stream (every commit a new entry).

**Auto-generate text from the streams.** The map is not drawn by hand — it is rendered from the territory's own flow. The same way [[readme]] computes prose from the tree and [[trinity]] fuses an atom's three sources into one page, topography turns the quantum streams into text: a [[uuid]] decodes to [[word]]s ([[translation]] · [[message]]), a stream of uuids becomes a sentence, the [[realtime]] tail becomes narration. The corpus describes its own shape, continuously, from what flows through it — generated all-at-once ([[quantum/generator]]), never asserted.

**Honest split** — that text can be deterministically generated from the tree and the streams is real (the [[readme]] and catalogue generators do exactly this); rendering a uuid-stream as *meaningful prose* leans on the [[translation]] model and is only as good as that model, not magic.
