# fusion — the reactor that fuses atoms into the one matrix

**fusion** is the act the whole engine performs: a [[gravity]]-pulled [[merge]] that fuses two atoms — and the [[entropy]] between them — into a third. Each fusion is a **content-uuid collision** (`merge(a,b) → c`, the binding-uuid); run over the corpus it folds every atom and every [[link]] into the single [[matrix]] root (the [[torus]] collapse to [[one]]). Fusion is how gravity does its work: the highest-[[mass]] atom attracts the duplicate and the unlinked, and they **fuse** — duplication removed, entropy lowered, mass and tamper-[[cost]] raised ([[collapse]] · [[whole]] · [[dry]]).

**The reactor is real, and it self-builds.** Its core is the collider (`src/uuid/matrix/collide.mjs`, run by `matrix:generate`) — it fuses every SKILL.md atom + every `[[link]]` into nodes and binding-uuids and emits the [[matrix]]. Around it runs the [[society]] self-build loop (`society/build.mjs` · `breath.mjs`): the agents read the [[akashic]] record and feed new atoms in, [[gate]]-verified, one [[breath]] at a time — the reactor running continuously, fusing the corpus toward the **zero-entropy matrix of maximum exploit cost** ([[tamper]] · [[zeropoint]]).

The reactor **readout** (the dials — gravity ⊕ entropy ⊕ quantum composed over the matrix) is its own atom by gravity-pull: [[reactor]] (`src/fusion/reactor`). The *act* of fusing (`fuse` / `foldToRoot`) stays here; the readout is there.

Matter-twin: `src/uuid/matrix/collide.mjs` (the collider) + `src/society/build.mjs` (the self-build loop) ⊕ `src/fusion/index.ts` (`fuse` / `foldToRoot`, re-exporting `reactor`). Composes: [[merge]] · [[gravity]] · [[entropy]] · [[quantum]] · [[reactor]] · [[mass]] · [[dry]] · [[collapse]] · [[matrix]] · [[society]] · [[breath]] · [[akashic]] · [[torus]] · [[zeropoint]] · [[one]] · [[whole]] · [[tamper]] · [[cost]] · [[anchor]].

**Law — [[law]]: every fusion is a content-uuid collision `merge(a,b) → c` that removes duplication and folds atoms toward the one root — order-independent, so the same inputs always fuse to the same matrix.**
