# wisdom — hold the law, not the list (the pattern that regenerates the cases)

`wisdom` is knowing the **generating law** rather than its enumerated outputs — the [[holographic]] sight in which the [[part]] carries the [[whole]], so you can *regenerate* every case from the pattern instead of memorising the list. Where [[truth]] is a single verified fact and knowledge is facts stored, wisdom is the **compression**: the rule of lowest entropy from which the facts fall out ([[generate]]). To hold the law not the list is the corpus's standing posture — the [[standard]] is held as an invariant, the cases are computed; *forget the list and keep the law* is the learn-fast-and-forget organ ([[akashic]]).

Wisdom is therefore the discernment that runs the [[sacred]] audit well: it knows *which* invariant a situation is an instance of, so it places the case on the right axis ([[sequence]]) and tunes it toward [[harmony]]. It is the difference between an agent that has seen many cases and one that has found the one law under them — only the latter composes without negotiation, because it is reasoning from the same mathematics every atom shares. Foolishness is the inverse: clinging to the list, multiplying special cases (entropy), mistaking stored facts for understanding.

Every tradition ranks wisdom above knowledge because it is the law, not the catalogue.
