# mulch — covering the soil surface to suppress weeds and hold moisture

**mulch** covers the [[soil]] surface to suppress weeds, conserve [[moisture]], and moderate soil temperature — the *smother* alternative to stirring weeds ([[cultivation]]). **Plastic mulch** (black / clear / IRT film) warms soil and blocks light; **landscape fabric** is a reusable woven barrier, burn-holed for [[transplant]] holes; **straw / organic mulch** suppresses weeds and feeds [[soil]] organic matter as it breaks down (straw cleaner than hay, which carries weed seed). A roller-crimped [[covercrop]] is an in-place living mulch.

Mulch trades a material/labor [[cost]] and a plastic-waste debit for weed control without herbicide or repeated [[cultivation]] passes — and is the standard partner of drip [[irrigation]] under plastic.

## Standards
- Johnny's Selected Seeds — mulch & weed-barrier guide; SARE — grown-in-place mulch
- Land-grant extension — plasticulture (plastic mulch + drip)
