# symbiosis — life as entanglement, not individuals (the biological market)

**Symbiosis** is the living-together of unlike organisms as one entangled system — and the central argument of *Entangled Life* is that it is the **rule, not the exception**: mycorrhizal fungi and plants ([[mycorrhizae]]), the fungus-and-alga of a [[lichen]], the bacteria that became our mitochondria (Margulis's endosymbiosis), the microbiome of every body (the **holobiont**). The lone organism is a convenient fiction; the real, durable unit is the **merge** of partners ([[merge]] · [[ecosystem]]).

**Mutualistic symbiosis is a market — and an honest one.** In the mycorrhizal symbiosis, plant **carbon** is traded for fungal **phosphorus** at *variable exchange rates*: partners that supply more get more in return, and cheaters that withhold are **sanctioned** (Kiers et al. 2011). That is erpax's economics exactly — an [[exchange]] that must [[balance]] ([[give]] ⇋ [[take]], a double-entry [[entry]]), a [[market]] whose reciprocal rewards are *enforced*, and a cheat caught the way a [[tamper]] is caught by the [[proof]]. Symbiosis is cooperation made **stable by accounting**, not by goodwill.

So fungi are nature's demonstration of the one [[law]]: the wired, reciprocal, **entangled** whole — not the isolated individual — is the real unit, and entanglement is not fragility but how life **persists** ([[sustainability]]) and **builds** ([[ecosystem]]). erpax's actor-[[merge]] (users, agents, organisms resolve to one) and its reciprocal all-directions wiring make the same claim, in code.

**Honest scope.** Symbiosis spans a continuum — mutualism (both gain) → commensalism → **parasitism** (one gains, one pays) — and the same partners can slide along it as conditions change; "living together" is not always "both win." The biological-market result (Kiers) is well-supported for arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi; the broader "everything is cooperation" reading is a lens, not a law.

## Standards
- **Kiers et al.** (2011, *Science*) — "Reciprocal rewards stabilize cooperation in the mycorrhizal symbiosis": carbon-for-phosphorus trade, variable exchange rates, sanctions. The biological market, peer-reviewed.
- **Margulis** — serial endosymbiosis: mitochondria and chloroplasts are domesticated bacteria; the holobiont as a unit of selection.
- **Sheldrake, *Entangled Life*** (2020) — symbiosis as the rule of life; "we are all lichens."
