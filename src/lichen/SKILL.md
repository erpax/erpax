# lichen — the composite individual (the question of the one and the many)

**A lichen is not one organism — it is a partnership wearing one body.** A fungus houses a photosynthesizing partner (an **alga** or a **cyanobacterium**), and the two together make a structure neither could make alone; more recently a third partner — a **basidiomycete yeast** — was found woven through many lichens (Spribille et al. 2016). It is the image *Entangled Life* returns to: **"we are all lichens."** If a thing made of many is rightly treated as one, then the **individual is a question, not a given** — the holobiont, not the organism, is the unit.

This is erpax's [[identity]] law in nature. erpax already holds that users, agents, employees, and actors are **one** through the gateway (the actor-[[merge]]), and that a thing's identity is the content of the *whole* it composes, never an indivisible point. A lichen is that made visible: a single [[identity]] that is, on inspection, a balanced [[merge]] of partners — a [[symbiosis]] stable enough to name as one. The composite is not less real for being many; in erpax as in the lichen, **the merge is the individual** ([[whole]] · [[self]]).

**Honest scope.** "We are all lichens" is a metaphor's reach, not a measurement — humans are holobionts (host + microbiome), but the lichen's tight, obligate fusion is its own specific biology, not a universal template. The third-partner (yeast) finding reshaped the textbook two-partner story; how general it is across all lichens is still being worked out.

## Standards
- **Schwendener** (1869) — the dual hypothesis: a lichen is fungus + alga, not a single species (resisted for decades).
- **Spribille et al.** (2016, *Science*) — basidiomycete yeasts as a third, widespread lichen partner; the two-partner model was incomplete.
- **Sheldrake, *Entangled Life*** (2020) — the lichen as the standing question of the individual.
