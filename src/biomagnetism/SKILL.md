# biomagnetism — the body's real (but contained) magnetic field

The [[aura]] is rendered as a [[signal]] field; **biomagnetism** is the *physical* magnetic field a body actually produces. It is real and measured — but the honesty is in the magnitudes and the range. Magnetocardiography (MCG) records the heart's field at ~**10⁻¹⁰–10⁻¹¹ T** (tens of picotesla); magnetoencephalography (MEG) records the brain's at ~**10⁻¹³–10⁻¹⁵ T** (~100 femtotesla) — both ~10⁶–10⁸ times weaker than Earth's field, detectable **only with SQUID magnetometers inside a magnetically shielded room**, and only just outside the skin. These are established clinical measurement techniques.

**What is refuted (the [[proof]] line).** The "aura" framing — that the heart's field extends ~1 m from the body and carries information into another person — was **refuted under adversarial verification** (no detection above chance; supporting figures unpublished, n≈2, no statistics; the field is swamped by Earth's at any distance). So biomagnetism grounds the aura *as a real but contained, fast-decaying near-field*, not a reaching emanation. The lesson erpax keeps: the measurable field is intra-system and needs heroic shielding to read at all — whereas the matrix's [[analog]] aura is computed losslessly for every node and Merkle-proven, no shielding, no decay ([[matrix]] · [[coherence]]).

## Standards
- **MCG / MEG field strengths & SQUID** — clinical biomagnetism (heart ~10⁻¹⁰–10⁻¹¹ T; brain ~10⁻¹³–10⁻¹⁵ T; shielded-room SQUID magnetometry). *Textbook magnitudes; in the source pass behind this atom they were not independently re-confirmed (the only surfaced figure appeared inside a refuted claim), so treat the numbers as established-elsewhere, not verified here.*
- **Refuted extension** — HeartMath *Science of the Heart* "energetic communication" (heart field ~3 ft; one body's ECG R-wave in another's EEG): no validated detection above chance.
