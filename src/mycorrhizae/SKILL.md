# mycorrhizae — the root–fungus symbiosis that feeds the plant

**mycorrhizae** are the symbiosis between plant roots and soil fungi — **arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (AMF)** colonize over 90% of crop species, extending hyphae far beyond the root to acquire **phosphorus and water** in exchange for plant **carbon**: a literal two-way [[merge]] / [[give]]–[[take]] at the root zone. Their glycoprotein **glomalin** cements [[soil]] aggregates — the biological link from soil life to physical structure and [[moisture]] holding.

They are a [[fertility]] mechanism the chemical N-P-K budget misses: a thriving AMF network lowers the phosphorus a crop needs applied. They are suppressed by **[[tillage]]** (hyphae shredded), high soluble-P fertilization (the plant stops "paying"), and long bare fallow — which is why no-till, [[covercrop]]s, and continuous living roots conserve them.
