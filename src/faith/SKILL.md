# faith — trust without verify (the dual of proof)

`faith` is the complement of [[proof]]: where proof is the O(N) trustless recompute (verify the bytes, trust no one), faith is trust extended *before* or *without* that recompute — accepting a source as canonical on its standing alone. erpax's bias is proof — *verify, don't trust*; the released forge≫verify asymmetry IS the trust — yet every system still spends faith where recompute is impossible or not-yet-run: trusting the [[standard]], the upstream source, another agent's [[contribution]] before auditing it. The two are a [[duality]]: all-faith-no-proof is forgeable ([[profane]] slips through); all-proof-no-faith never bootstraps — you must trust *some* root key. The healthy ratio is most-proof, least-faith — faith only at the irreducible root, everything above it verified.
