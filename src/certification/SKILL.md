# certification — third-party attestation of conformance to a standard

**certification** is the generic mechanism by which an accredited agent attests an operation conforms to a [[standards|standard]] and issues a transferable **claim** (a label/mark). The pattern recurs across schemes: **organic** ([[organic]] — USDA NOP, EU 2018/848), **biodynamic** (Demeter), **Regenerative Organic Certified** (soil/animal/worker pillars), **GLOBALG.A.P.** and USDA **GAP/GHP** (food-safety audits), and **participatory guarantee systems** (peer-verified, trust-based — the low-cost smallholder alternative to third-party audit, a [[governance]] form). Each is one shape: a standard + an accredited [[party|agent]] + an [[audit]] + a [[period|periodic]] re-review + a labeled claim.

Certification is where [[agriculture]] meets the trust layer: the claim is only as strong as the **record** that proves it — verifiable provenance ([[lineage]]/[[lots]]), tamper-evident history ([[proof]]/[[integrity]]). It generalizes far beyond food (ISO, the [[standards]] corpus itself), which is why it is its own atom rather than a fact buried in [[organic]].

## Standards
- USDA NOP accreditation (7 CFR 205 subpart F); IFOAM — PGS & organic guarantee systems
- Demeter Biodynamic Standard; Regenerative Organic Certified; GLOBALG.A.P. IFA; USDA AMS GAP/GHP
- ISO/IEC 17065 — conformity assessment (bodies certifying products & processes)
