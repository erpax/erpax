# age — the life-course axis along which advantage compounds (възраст)

**age** (*възраст*) is a [[dimension]] — an **axis** along which actors coexist at one [[society]], a point on the life-course [[sequence]], not a separate home. Young and old are **roles on one axis** (the dimension law: don't split the population into separate entities — carry age as the coordinate). It is a *master status* that stratifies [[society]] alongside class and [[status]], and it is the axis that defines the *late* in *late [[career]]*.

**Advantage compounds along it (the doubling).** Cumulative (dis)advantage (Dannefer): small early differences **compound** across the life course — the [[horo]] doubling, a [[power]]-law over [[time]] — so age **widens** inequality (the rich-get-richer of a lifetime). The unit of analysis is the **birth [[cohort]]**: generations *are* cohorts, and the classic decomposition separates age × period × cohort effects — never read a cross-section of old people as the future of the young without it.

**Ageism — the status sign flips.** In the labour market the [[status]] sign turns **negative** on age: the older worker is stigmatized (Butler's *ageism*), the same social-sign mechanism the [[market]] and [[status]] atoms model, here flipped by age. This conditions the late-career band ([[career]]) and **depresses re-employment under [[crisis]]** — the older a worker when the shock lands, the slower the recovery.

**Demographic ageing strains the balance (BG-native).** Bulgaria is among the fastest-ageing and shrinking populations on earth, so this is not abstract: the population pyramid **inverts**, the old-age dependency ratio rises (the EC *2021 Ageing Report* projects Bulgaria's 65+/20–64 ratio roughly **doubling — ≈36% (2019) → ≈67% (2058)** — with total population falling **≈28%, to ~5.0M by 2070**), and **fewer workers must fund more retirees** — a [[balance]] problem at society scale, because the conserved pay-pot of [[allocation]] / [[decompression]] now supports more claims from fewer contributors. The policy answer is *active / healthy ageing* (WHO): keep capability and [[health]] high across the late [[career]] so the conversion of years into freedom stays favourable. age thus conditions [[wellbeing]] (the health-and-capability gradient steepens late — Sen's conversion factors worsen), [[career]] (the late band), and exposure to [[crisis]].

## Standards
- **World Report on Ageing and Health / active ageing** — WHO.
- **Cumulative advantage/disadvantage** — Dale Dannefer; Matthew effect over the life course (Merton).
- **Ageism** — Robert Butler; EU age-discrimination directive (2000/78/EC).
- **Population ageing** — UN *World Population Ageing*; Eurostat old-age dependency ratio.
- **Bulgarian demography & pensions** — EC *2021 Ageing Report* (BG pension fiche): old-age dependency ratio ≈36%→≈67% by 2058, population −28% by 2070; statutory retirement age rising to 65 (2037) then life-expectancy-linked; effective retirement ≈63.5 (men)/62.6 (women).
