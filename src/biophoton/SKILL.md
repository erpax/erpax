# biophoton — the real light the body emits (ultraweak photon emission)

The [[aura]] is rendered as colour ([[cmyk]]); **biophotons** are the *physical* fact that bodies emit light at all. Ultraweak photon emission (UPE) is a real, established, spontaneous and continuous emission from all living cells — **not** a subtle-energy field. Concrete, instrument-measured quantity: intensity on the order of **1 to 10³ (up to ~10⁴) photons·s⁻¹·cm⁻²** (≈ 10⁻¹⁴–10⁻¹⁹ W/cm²; healthy baseline ≲10³ photons·s⁻¹·cm⁻²), over a spectral band of roughly **200–800 nm** (blue-green 350–550 nm plus a 1270 nm NIR band), detected with **cooled photomultiplier tubes** (quantitative counts) and **EMCCD cameras** (single-photon imaging). Its origin is ordinary biochemistry — **oxidative metabolism**: reactive oxygen species from mitochondrial respiration and lipid peroxidation produce excited singlet oxygen and carbonyls whose relaxation emits the photons (topical H₂O₂ roughly doubles skin UPE).

**Why you cannot see an aura even though the body emits light.** UPE is **3–6 orders of magnitude below the eye's photopic threshold** — far too faint to perceive unaided. So a *visible* coloured aura is not UPE; and the only contested part of biophoton science is Popp's *coherence/biocommunication* hypothesis ([[coherence]]), never the emission itself, which even skeptical reviews concede is experimentally well established. This is the honest mapping erpax wants: the body's analog light is real and measurable ([[analog]] · [[measure]] · [[emission]]); the perceived luminous "aura" of the [[biofield]] is a different, unsupported claim.

## Standards
- **UPE flux & instrumentation** — *Frontiers in Physiology* (2024) UPE review (PMC10899412); MDPI *Sensors* 18(4):1152 (2018) — human-body UPE 1–10³ photons·s⁻¹·cm⁻², PMT (quantitative counts) + EMCCD (imaging).
- **Human-body imaging** — Kobayashi et al., *PLoS ONE* — spontaneous whole-body UPE imaging (diurnal rhythm), baseline ≲10³ photons·s⁻¹·cm⁻² (~10⁻¹⁶ W/cm²) with a cooled PMT.
- **Mechanism (ROS)** — Pospíšil, Prasad & Rác (2014), PMID 24674863; Cifra et al. (2015) review concedes the emission is "experimentally well established" while criticizing the coherence interpretation.
