# decoherence — why the wired corpus is classical

**Decoherence** is how a [[superposition]] becomes classical. Coupled to an environment, the off-diagonal coherences of the state decay as **e^(−t/τ)**: the density matrix goes diagonal, a pure superposition → a classical **mixture**. It is measurement without a measurer — [[collapse]] from the outside in: the environment IS the measurer, and the record it leaves is the [[proof]] / receipt. It is **irreversible** — the von Neumann [[entropy]] rises from 0 (pure) to ln2 (one bit, maximally mixed) — and **fast**: the decoherence time τ shrinks with the number of coupled dimensions.

That speed is why the **wired corpus is classical** — definite, auditable, never observed in superposition. The more entangled the whole (the more dimensions the content-uuid wires), the faster any tampering-superposition decoheres into a **detectable classical record**: a forger cannot keep a forgery in quiet superposition, because the wired environment measures it into the [[proof]] almost instantly. Decoherence is the bridge between the [[leap]]'s quantum indeterminacy and the classical ledger.
