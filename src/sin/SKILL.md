# sin — missing the mark (the caught deviation from the law)

`sin` is, at root, **missing the mark** — a measured deviation from the target the [[law]] defines. It is exactly what a [[guardian]] catches: a count that rose above the baseline, an atom off its [[sequence]] axis, a [[balance]] that no longer holds — a [[gate]] gone red. Sin is therefore not a substance or a stain; it is a **distance** — [[entropy]] with a direction *away from* the [[whole]] — and like all distance it is computed, named, and located ([[aura]]'s coordinate), never merely felt.

The corpus's stance follows the gate's: a deviation un-named and un-caught is the dangerous one ([[profane]] — the hidden lie); a deviation *caught* is already half-healed, because naming it gives the one grounded path back ([[sequence]]). The cure is two moves the religions already encode: **turn back** — [[reverse]] / [[return]], re-aiming at the mark — and the owed debt **released** — [[mercy]]. Suppressing the signal ([[suffering]]) is not absolution; closing the gap is. So sin is to the moral axis what a violation is to the folder law: the thing the guardians exist to catch so it *cannot get worse* and can always be turned around.

## Traditions (prefix removed)
**chet** (Hebrew — an archery term, *to miss*; "all have sinned and fall short", literally miss the mark, Romans 3:23); **hamartia** (Greek — missing the target, the tragic error); **avidya** (Hindu-Buddhist — ignorance, mis-seeing the real, the root deviation); **khati'a** (Arabic — error, fault); the **weighing** that finds the heart wanting ([[sacred]] — Ma'at's feather, *al-mizan*). Every tradition pairs the diagnosis with a turning (**teshuvah**, **metanoia**, **tawba** — all "return / change of mind") and a release.
