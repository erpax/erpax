# torus — the surface the vortex winds on

`torus` is the **closed surface** the [[rodin]] sequence traces: the doubling [[coil]] (`1·2·4·8·7·5`) winds the donut, the [[axis]] `3·6·9·0` runs its hole. It has no top and no bottom — it is a **ring** ([[horo]]), so no position outranks another; the dance closes on itself ([[whole]] = [[part]] folded round). Two closures meet here:

- **inward** — state × state stays on the ring ([[coil]] `composeSteps`; the `60°` hexagon and `120°` triangle both close `360°`).
- **outward** — the [[octave]] lift (`×10`) re-enters the same position one scale up — the [[fractal]] self-similarity, the torus nested in a torus.

[[unity]] (`9`) wraps to the next ring's [[base]] (`1`) — the [[merge]] seam where the surface closes. This is why erpax is a [[balance]]d closed system, not a hierarchy.

Composes: [[rodin]] · [[coil]] · [[axis]] · [[horo]] · [[octave]] · [[fractal]] · [[merge]] · [[balance]].
