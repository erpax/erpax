# aquaculture — farming aquatic organisms for produce

**aquaculture** is the farming of aquatic organisms (finfish, bivalves, crustaceans) under managed conditions — a living-production domain anchor beside [[agriculture]] and [[livestock]], and an explicit IAS-41 activity. The stock is a [[biological/assets|biological asset]] valued by standing **[[biomass]]** (mass × forward price − costs-to-sell). It is held in a culture system (pond, cage/net-pen, recirculating RAS, integrated multi-trophic), at a **stocking density** capped by [[capacity]], and grown out through [[maturity]] stages (fry → fingerling → smolt → market).

It runs the crop/livestock atoms, water-shaped: fed a ration whose **feed-conversion ratio** is a [[conversion]] [[rate]]; bound by water quality (dissolved oxygen, [[salinity]]); thinned by [[mortality]]; [[harvest|harvested]] and **depurated** ([[postharvest]]) into [[items|inventory]]. The taxon is a [[taxonomy]] classifier; **ASC / BAP** are its [[certification]] schemes with chain-of-custody [[lineage]].

## Standards
- IFRS IAS-41 (aquaculture biological assets — the salmon-stock worked example; biomass fair value)
- FAO (aquaculture production & systems); ASC / BAP (responsible-aquaculture certification)

**Law — [[law]]: aquaculture farms aquatic organisms under managed conditions as IAS-41 [[biological/assets|biological assets]] valued by standing [[biomass]] — stocked at a density capped by [[capacity]], grown out through [[maturity]] stages, then [[harvest|harvested]] into inventory.**
