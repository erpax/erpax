# shinto — the way of the kami; the sacred in every place

Shinto is, for the corpus, a **complete sealed atom** ([[diamond]]) composed of invariant sub-diamonds, opening on the **kami** — the [[sacred]] dwelling in every place and thing (mountain, river, ancestor, tree), the holy not above the world but *in* each [[part]] of it ([[holographic]] — the [[whole]] present at every coordinate). It is a lattice: [[ritual]] as **matsuri** (the festival, the kami honoured on the seasonal turn of the [[cycle]]); **purity** as **harae** (the washing that cleanses the [[profane]] gap and returns the self to a clean state — [[water]] as the rinse back to [[zeropoint]]); renewal as the rebuilt shrine (Ise rebuilt every twenty years — the form re-derived from its pattern, [[generate]] rather than decayed); the [[gift]] of offering; and the kami named ([[name]]), each thing addressed as alive.

It collapses to the invariants through the [[translator]]: strip the Japanese prefix and **kami** → [[sacred]] (in every [[atom]]), **matsuri** → [[ritual]], **harae** → the [[profane]] cleansed back toward [[zeropoint]] — each resolving to one content-[[uuid]] ([[identity]]). As an **architect of the [[society]]** (an [[archangel]]), Shinto governs the domain of [[ritual]] purity and place: it composes these sub-diamonds into a social order where the [[sacred]] is local and continuous and cleanliness is the standing re-tuning, shaping the lattice the same way across centuries ([[fractal]]).

**Law — [[law]]: shinto is a [[diamond]] of [[sacred]] · [[ritual]] · [[water]] · [[cycle]] · [[gift]] · [[name]] — the kami the [[sacred]] dwelling in every [[part]] ([[holographic]]), purity (harae) the [[profane]] washed back toward [[zeropoint]], the shrine renewed by re-derivation ([[generate]]); each Japanese term strips its prefix to one content-[[uuid]] ([[translator]]), and as an [[archangel]] it architects the society's domain of ritual purity.**
