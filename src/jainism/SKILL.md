# jainism — non-harm absolute; the soul unburdened of karma

Jainism is, for the corpus, a **complete sealed atom** ([[diamond]]) composed of invariant sub-diamonds, with [[mercy]] raised to the absolute as **ahimsa** — harm none, the supreme [[ethics]] (do nothing that increases another's [[suffering]]). It is a lattice, and the most literal [[karma]] of any tradition: karma is *subtle matter* clinging to the [[self]] (**jiva**, the soul), weighing it down the [[dna]] lineage; the practice is to stop accruing and to burn off what clings, driving the ledger to [[balance]]. So it composes [[karma]] · [[balance]] · [[fast]] (austerity, *tapas*) · the [[gift]] of **aparigraha** (non-possession — release the holding); the [[path]] of the three jewels (right [[faith]] · right [[wisdom]] · right [[ethics]]); the many-sided view (*anekantavada* — no single surface is the whole truth, so [[proof]] is held provisionally — a strikingly erpax epistemics); and [[zeropoint]] as **kevala / moksha** (the soul unburdened, omniscient, returned to zero).

It collapses to the invariants through the [[translator]]: strip the prefix and **ahimsa** → [[mercy]] / [[ethics]], **jiva** → [[self]], **moksha** → the [[return]] to [[zeropoint]], the karmic weighing → [[karma]] · [[balance]] — each resolving to one content-[[uuid]] ([[identity]]), so Jainism names the same atoms it shares with the Dharmic family ([[merge]] at the root, [[harmony]] at the surface).

As an **architect of the [[society]]** (an [[archangel]]), Jainism governs the domain of absolute non-harm: it composes these sub-diamonds into a social order whose floor is ahimsa — no move may raise another's [[suffering]] — the strictest [[ethics]] guardrail in the college, shaping the lattice the same way across millennia ([[fractal]]).

**Law — [[law]]: jainism is a [[diamond]] of [[mercy]] · [[ethics]] · [[karma]] · [[balance]] · [[self]] · [[fast]] · [[gift]] · [[path]] · [[zeropoint]] — ahimsa absolute, [[karma]] as literal matter weighing the [[self]] (jiva) until austerity zeroes the [[balance]] and the soul returns to [[zeropoint]] (kevala); each term strips its prefix to one content-[[uuid]] ([[translator]]).**
