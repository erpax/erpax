# hinduism — the eternal law; the self is the whole

Hinduism is, for the corpus, a **complete sealed atom** ([[diamond]]) — **sanatana dharma**, the eternal [[law]] beneath all dialects — composed of invariant sub-diamonds. Its keystone is the identity of [[self]] and [[whole]]: **atman is brahman** (*tat tvam asi*, "that thou art") — the part *is* the whole read at one vertex, exactly the [[holographic]] law that a diamond is its own [[identity]]. It is a lattice: [[karma]] (the moral ledger inherited down the [[dna]] lineage, order created over destroyed); the [[cycle]] of rebirth (**samsara**, the turning [[wheel]]); release as [[return]] to [[zeropoint]] (**moksha**); [[prayer]] as attunement (**japa / dhyana** — the repeated [[name]], **om** the primal [[word]] from which the many spring); [[breath]] (**prana** — the vital breath); [[ritual]] (**yajna** — the [[fire]] offering on the turning [[cycle]]); and [[love]] as devotion (**bhakti** — the bond to the source). The many gods are [[name]]s of [[one]] (the perennial root beneath the surface forms).

It collapses to the invariants through the [[translator]]: strip the Sanskrit prefix and **dharma** → [[law]], **atman** → [[self]], **brahman** → [[whole]], **om** → [[word]], **prana** → [[breath]], **moksha** → the [[return]] to [[zeropoint]], **karuna** → [[mercy]] — each resolving to one content-[[uuid]] ([[identity]]), so Hinduism names the same atoms in Sanskrit ([[merge]] at the root, [[harmony]] at the surface).

As an **architect of the [[society]]** (an [[archangel]]), Hinduism governs the domain of [[self]] = [[whole]] and the [[karma]] ledger: it composes these sub-diamonds into a social order in which every part holds the whole ([[holographic]]) and every deed is booked down the lineage — shaping the lattice the same way across millennia ([[fractal]]).
