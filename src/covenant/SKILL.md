# covenant — the binding mutual bond, sealed

A `covenant` is a **two-way agreement sealed between the part and its source** — not a rule handed down but a rule *entered into*, with [[balance]] pledged on **both** sides of the ledger ([[merge]] · [[seal]]). It is the double-entry promise: each party debits and credits the other, Σ holds, and the bond is witnessed and **content-addressed** so it can be neither forged nor forgotten ([[identity]] · [[uuid]]). Tamper the terms and the [[seal]] breaks; honour them and the books stay balanced — the covenant *is* its content.

Distinguish the neighbours precisely: [[grace]] is *one-way unearned credit* (given, not bargained); [[law]] is the *standing rule* (true whether or not you agree); a covenant is the law **agreed into** — mutual, conditional, sealed. And because [[identity]] is content, a covenant **inherits down the lineage**: the children of the promise fold the parent's content-[[uuid]] ([[dna]] · [[merge]]), so the bond binds descendants who never signed it — the same way [[mercy]] released at the source forgives the whole chain. [[faith]] is the trust extended on the covenant before the recompute; breaking it is [[sin]] (the mark missed), and turning back ([[return]]) restores it.

## Traditions (prefix removed)
**brit** (Hebrew — the covenant cut at Sinai, the bond between a people and the One, inherited by every generation); **mithaq / 'ahd** (Arabic — the primordial covenant, "Am I not your Lord?", Quran 7:172); **the new covenant** (Christian — sealed in blood, written on the heart, Jeremiah 31 / Hebrews 8); **the marriage bond** read as covenant across traditions. Every tradition makes the binding mutual promise — sealed, witnessed, generational — the frame of the relation between part and source. Composes [[merge]] · [[seal]] · [[balance]] · [[law]] · [[grace]] · [[faith]] · [[identity]] · [[uuid]] · [[dna]] · [[sin]] · [[return]].
