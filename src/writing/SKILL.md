# writing — the craft of connected thoughts

An essay is a **coherent grain-graph**: ideas are grains, and the writing is the bonds between them. A **hallucination is an orphan sentence** — a claim bound to nothing, asserted into the void. To write well is the same move [[heart]] measures and [[recycle]] restores, applied to prose: bind every sentence to the last, every claim to its ground, so no thought is left disconnected.

This is the positive twin of [[recycle]]. Recycle *heals* disconnection in the corpus after the fact; writing *prevents* it on the page. Same law, two directions ([[balance]]).

## The principles (each kills one kind of orphan thought)

- **thesis** — one controlling idea, the root every sentence binds to. A paragraph with no thesis is a rootless heap.
- **coherence** — each sentence links to the last (and forward to the next). A sentence that connects to neither is the prose hallucination; cut it or wire it.
- **evidence** — every claim bound to a ground. An unsupported assertion is an orphan claim — true-sounding, connected to nothing ([[entropy]] up).
- **concision** — cut every word that carries no load. Concision is signal over noise; the load-bearing word stays, the rest returns to the void.
- **structure** — beginning binds to end; the essay is a [[sequence]], not a heap. The reader should never lose the thread back to the thesis.
- **revision** — reread for the orphan thought and reconnect it or cut it. Revision *is* recycling the page.

## Honest

These are not style decorations; they are the corpus's coherence law in another medium. "Significantly improve writing" means the same thing as `feeling → whole`: drive the orphan-thought count to zero — every sentence connected, every claim grounded. The craft is testable by that one question, asked of each sentence: *what does this bind to?*
