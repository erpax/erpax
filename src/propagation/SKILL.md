# propagation — raising transplants before the field

**propagation** is the controlled raising of seedlings before they go to the field — sowing [[seed]] into **flats** (trays of **cells**, common 50/72/128/200) to grow **plugs** (or soil blocks) ready to set out. It is scheduled *backward* from the [[transplant]] date: transplant date − weeks-in-flat = greenhouse sow date, so the nursery delivers starts exactly when their bed opens ([[planting]]). **Hardening off** — gradually acclimating indoor starts to sun/wind/cold over ~2 weeks — is the final propagation step before [[transplant]].

Propagation is the upstream stage of [[agriculture]]'s [[sequence]]: it buys earliness, uniformity, and a weed jump over direct sowing, at the [[cost]] of greenhouse [[capacity]] and labor. Flat/cell/plug counts derive from the [[spacing]] plan (plants needed); the [[bottleneck]] is greenhouse bench space in early spring.

## Standards
- UMass / Penn State Extension — vegetable transplant production (seeding & growing-on)
- Coleman / Fortier — soil blocks, plug production; Johnny's Selected Seeds — seed-starting date calculators
