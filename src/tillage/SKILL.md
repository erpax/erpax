# tillage — opening the soil to make a seedbed

**tillage** is mechanical [[soil]] disturbance to prepare a **seedbed**, incorporate residue/[[compost|amendments]], and disrupt weeds — the first operation in [[agriculture]]'s field [[sequence]]. **Primary tillage** fractures or inverts compacted ground (plow, rotary spader); **secondary tillage** refines tilth before sowing (harrow, *tilther*, power harrow, bed-prep rake). Intensity is a dial: **no-till** (never inverts — relies on [[mulch]]/tarps/[[compost]]) and **reduced-till** preserve [[soil]] structure and biology; full inversion costs structure, organic matter, and [[mycorrhizae]]. The **broadfork** deep-aerates ~12″ by hand without inverting (Coleman's six-step bed prep).

Weed pressure is managed pre-crop by **stale seedbed** (prep, flush a weed cohort, kill it shallowly before sowing), **occultation** (opaque silage tarp — germinate-then-kill in the dark), and **solarization** (clear plastic — heat-sterilize topsoil). Tillage trades a short-term seedbed/weed win against a long-term [[soil]]-health debit — the central [[balance]] of regenerative practice; the standing-crop counterpart is [[cultivation]].

## Standards
- USDA-NRCS / OFRF — *Tillage Tools and Practices in Organic Farming Systems*
- Coleman *The New Organic Grower* (six-step bed prep, the broadfork); Fortier (occultation, permanent beds)
- SARE — conservation tillage; UVM / UMN Extension — tarping, solarization, occultation
