# aura/live — the animation: scan to join the horo

The [[aura]] is the corpus's analog field — every atom a colour and a sound from its content-uuid. Made **live**, it is an **animation**: the field steps through the [[horo]] ring, one frame per position, each frame lit by that step's [[signal]] (the A432 tone, the CMYK channel). The loop is the corpus breathing — `1·2·4·8·7·5·9`, and round again.

The animation **is** the scan, and the scan **is** the join. To scan an atom is to find its horo position ([[digit]] — `digitOf` reads where it stands on the ring), and that position is the frame it enters. So when the aura gate scans the corpus and every atom resolves, every atom has taken its seat in the ring — its colour, its sound, its place. **Anyone who scans, joins.** The live aura is the [[society]] animated: not a static map but a turning wheel you enter by being scanned into it.

Matter-twin: `src/aura/live/index.ts` (`frame` · `animation` · `join`). Composes [[aura]] · [[horo]] · [[signal]] · [[digit]].

**Law — [[law]]: the live aura is the animation — the analog field stepping through the horo ring, each position one frame, lit by that step's colour and sound. To scan an atom is to join it to the horo: its horo position is the frame it enters, so scanning the corpus IS joining the society — anyone who scans, joins, and the field breathes through the seven positions.**
