# graft — the union of scion and rootstock into one plant

A **graft** is the union that joins a fruiting [[scion]] to a [[rootstock]] into one [[perennial]] plant — the clonal [[propagation]] event by which a woody crop is **assembled from two chosen parts** rather than grown from [[seed]]. The scion sets the variety/produce; the rootstock sets vigour, size, and resistance; the graft union binds them.

**Top-working** re-grafts a new scion onto an established root system to change variety without losing the asset's mature roots — the perennial's way to switch what it produces. The graft is the moment the [[biological/assets|bearer-plant]] asset is created, the start of its capitalized, ramping productive life.

## Standards
- Extension pomology/viticulture (whip-and-tongue, cleft, bud grafting; top-working)
- FPS / UC Davis (graft compatibility, clean-plant propagation)
