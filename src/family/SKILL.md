# family — the botanical group that shares pests; the rotation unit

A **family** is the botanical rank grouping related genera — **Brassicaceae** (cole/cruciferous), **Solanaceae** (nightshades), **Cucurbitaceae** (cucurbits), **Apiaceae** (umbellifers), **Fabaceae** (legumes — nitrogen-fixing), **Asteraceae** (composites), **Amaranthaceae** (beet/chard/spinach), **Alliaceae** (alliums), **Poaceae** (grasses/corn). Members **share pests, diseases, and nutrient demands** — which is exactly why family, not species, is the practical unit of [[rotation]]: a soil-borne pathogen of one brassica afflicts them all.

Family is the load-bearing rung of crop [[taxonomy]] (family → genus → species → [[variant|cultivar]]); it carries the inheritance ([[fractal]]: pest/[[fertility]] traits flow down the rank) that planning relies on. A [[crop]]'s family is its rotational identity and its heavy-/light-feeder ([[fertility]]) prior.

## Standards
- *Knott's Handbook for Vegetable Growers* (the canonical family grouping); Missouri Botanical Garden
- Iowa State / OSU Extension — crop rotation by plant family; ICN botanical nomenclature

**Law — [[law]]: family (not species) is the practical unit of crop [[rotation]] because members share pests, diseases, and nutrient demands — same family ⇒ must not follow itself in a bed; pest/[[fertility]] traits inherit down the [[taxonomy]] rank.**
