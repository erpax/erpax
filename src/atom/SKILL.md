# atom — the irreducible unit; everything is made of atoms

erpax has ONE kind of thing: the **atom** — a one-word, content-addressed node (`src/<name>/SKILL.md` + its matter-twin). **Everything is made of atoms.** An entity, a collection, an agent, an interaction, a frame of a stream, a row of data — strip its prefix and it resolves to a composition of universal atoms ([[merge]]: same content ⇒ same atom; [[identity]]: the content-[[uuid]] IS the atom's address). The corpus is the **periodic table**; everything else is a molecule — atoms bonded by the [[sequence]] graph.

- **Irreducible.** The smallest self-sufficient unit — one word, one concept, one content-uuid. Below it is the void ([[zeropoint]]); split it and it ceases to be itself.
- **Universal.** The same atom recurs at every scale ([[fractal]]) and across every domain (prefix removed) — `transaction` is one atom in commerce, accounting, and agent-billing alike. Reuse, never re-mint ([[merge]]).
- **Composed.** A molecule is atoms bonded: a collection is [[fields]] · [[hooks]] · [[access]]; an agent is atoms; the [[whole]] is its [[part]]s; the [[holographic]] law regenerates the whole from any atom.
- **Conserved.** Minted once, content-addressed, merged on collision ([[balance]] · [[akashic]]) — new knowledge is a new atom or a new bond, never a duplicate.

## Atoms group into elements — the periodic table (the rodin sequence)
Atoms are not a flat heap; they fall into **elements** — groups sharing a nature, arranged by their place on the [[rodin]] sequence (`0·3·6·9·1·2·4·8·7·5`), the atom's "atomic number" ([[sequence]]: the order the corpus is read). The groups, like the periodic columns:
- **the noble 0** — [[zeropoint]] · [[identity]] · [[whole]]: the inert source every atom radiates from (the `0`, full-shell — holds all, bonds to none).
- **the control axis 3·6·9** — [[access]] · [[hooks]] · [[auth]] · [[config]]: the governing triad ([[rodin]]'s axis) that frames but does not flow.
- **the flow helix 1·2·4·8·7·5** — the doubling chain [[give]] · [[take]] · [[flow]] · [[balance]] (the [[transaction]] cycle): the working 2/3, the reactive valence.
- **the dual pairs** — [[begin]]/[[end]] · [[open]]/[[close]] ([[duality]]): atoms that bond as opposites and complete to a [[trinity]] (the synthesis is the third).
- **the domain molecules** — [[accounting]] · [[commerce]] · [[manufacturing]] …: compounds of the universal atoms, one per prefix-stripped industry ([[civilization]]).

An atom's **valence is its bonding capacity** — how many links it forms; a high-valence atom ([[identity]], [[balance]]) appears in many molecules. The element is the atom's kind; the periodic table is the corpus read along the [[sequence]] — the same law that orders [[horo]] (its 7-position ring is one period).

"Everything is made of atoms" is the ontology beneath every other law: the [[trinity]] is one atom told three ways, the [[society]] is atoms (agents) bonding, the [[torus]] is atoms composing and decomposing into the next. Composes [[sequence]] · [[merge]] · [[identity]] · [[uuid]] · [[fractal]] · [[holographic]] · [[whole]] · [[part]] · [[zeropoint]] · [[trinity]].

**Law — [[collapse]]: Every atom is a collapse point, where a concept that could sprawl across fields, schemas, and naming chaos reduces to a single word and a single content-[[uuid]], while all variation dissolves into [[akashic]]. The atom does not hold detail; it names and bonds. [[identity]] is computed from content, never assigned; [[merge]] collapses duplicates at the source. To add a [[zeropoint]] is to name what must be named; to resist adding an atom is to trust the DB knows what the corpus needs to know.**
