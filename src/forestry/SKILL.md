# forestry — managing standing timber for produce (and carbon)

**forestry** is the management of standing trees' biological transformation for timber and other produce — a living-production domain anchor beside [[agriculture]] and [[aquaculture]], explicitly in IAS-41 scope. A **stand** of trees is a [[biological/assets|biological asset]] valued by standing **[[biomass]]** / merchantable volume at fair-value-less-costs-to-sell (the *land* is a separate asset); felling is the [[harvest]] event that turns the standing crop into log [[items|inventory]].

Forestry reuses the crop atoms at a decadal scale: the **rotation age** (set by mean annual increment) is the [[rotation]] atom; **silviculture** (tending) is [[cultivation]], **regeneration** is [[propagation]], **thinning** and clearcut are [[harvest]] methods; **site index** (land productivity) is [[fertility]]; growth-and-yield models [[forecast]] the [[biomass]]; [[mortality]] writes it down. **Carbon stock / sequestration** is a cross-cutting ESG output (carbon credits). **FSC / PEFC** are its [[certification]] schemes with chain-of-custody [[lineage]]; rights are [[tenure]].

## Standards
- IFRS IAS-41 (timber as a biological asset — the forest worked example); FAO SFM
- USDA Forest Service / SAF (rotation age, MAI, silviculture); FSC / PEFC (sustainable-forestry certification)
