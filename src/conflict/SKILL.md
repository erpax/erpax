# conflict — the adversity collision (the merge law as a clearance check)

Before a [[matter]] opens, the firm must prove there is no **conflict**: the proposed [[party]] (client) must not be adverse to a party the firm already serves. Strip the prefix and this is the [[merge]] / [[identity]] law as a clearance gate — the same entity resolves to the same content-uuid everywhere ([[identity]]), so a search of the [[party]] relationship graph for the proposed client and its related entities either finds a collision (adverse representation — the matter is barred or walled) or clears. It is **detection, not prevention** ([[proof]] surfaces the collision; the [[society]] decides — erpax detects adversity, judgment resolves it). A cleared check is recorded ([[akashic]]); a found conflict routes to a [[privilege]] wall or a declination. Composes [[merge]] · [[identity]] · [[party]] · [[matter]] · [[privilege]] · [[proof]].
